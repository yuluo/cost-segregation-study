# Professional Standards and Ethics

## ASCSP Overview

The American Society of Cost Segregation Professionals (ASCSP) serves as the premier professional organization for cost segregation practitioners.

### Mission
- Establish and maintain professional standards
- Promote best practices in cost segregation
- Provide education and certification for practitioners
- Advocate for the profession

### Membership Categories

#### Technical Members
- Directly involved in cost segregation studies
- Must have technical expertise (engineering, accounting, tax)
- Responsible for study quality and accuracy

#### Non-Technical Members
- Support roles (marketing, administration)
- Do not perform technical aspects of studies
- Contribute to industry advancement

## Code of Ethics

### Core Principles
- **Integrity**: Maintain honesty and truthfulness in all professional activities
- **Objectivity**: Avoid conflicts of interest and bias
- **Professional Competence**: Maintain knowledge and skill at required levels
- **Confidentiality**: Respect confidential information
- **Professional Behavior**: Comply with laws and regulations

### Professional Responsibilities
- Perform work with due professional care
- Maintain independence and objectivity
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Report findings accurately and completely

## Minimum Quality Standards (MQS)

### Key Requirements
- Proper documentation and support
- Appropriate methodology application
- Qualified personnel involvement
- Comprehensive reporting
- Independent review processes

### Study Components
- Site inspection and documentation
- Engineering analysis
- Cost analysis and allocation
- Tax law application
- Quality control review

### Compliance
- Members must adhere to MQS for all studies
- Regular updates to reflect changes in tax law and practice
- Enforcement through membership review
