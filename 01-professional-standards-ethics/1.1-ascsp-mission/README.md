# The ASCSP Mission

## Introduction
The American Society of Cost Segregation Professionals (ASCSP) is a non-profit organization providing organizational oversight for the cost segregation industry.

## Primary Goal
Establish measurable standards for business practices and deliverable quality in cost segregation studies.

## Core Objectives

### Educational Programs
- Develop and maintain comprehensive training programs
- Provide continuing education for professionals

### Minimum Quality Standards
- Define standard practices for conducting cost segregation studies
- Establish benchmarks for report quality and completeness

### Code of Ethics Implementation
- Enforce professional conduct standards
- Maintain integrity across the industry

## Key Developments

### Credentials Based on Education and Experience
- Tiered membership system reflecting expertise levels
- Experience requirements ensuring qualified practitioners
- Educational prerequisites for technical designations

### Cost Segregation Protocol Approval
- Standardized methodologies for conducting studies
- Quality assurance processes
- Peer review mechanisms

### Training and Certification Procedures
- Structured examination process
- Member and Certified Professional designations
- Ongoing professional development requirements

## Industry Role

### Communication with IRS
- While not formally recognized by the IRS, ASCSP serves as a liaison
- Provides industry perspective on cost segregation matters
- Advocates for professional standards

### Thought Leader
- Sets industry best practices
- Advances the professional standing of cost segregation
- Promotes standardization across the field
