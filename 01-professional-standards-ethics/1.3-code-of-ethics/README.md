# Code of Ethics

## Purpose
The Code of Ethics governs the professional conduct of all ASCSP members to safeguard:
- **Clients** receiving cost segregation services
- **The public** relying on professional standards
- **The profession** maintaining credibility and integrity

## Fundamental Principles

### Integrity
- Maintain honesty in all professional relationships
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Uphold the reputation of the profession

### Honesty
- Provide truthful and accurate representations
- Disclose limitations and uncertainties
- Communicate findings objectively

### Objectivity
- Remain impartial in professional judgments
- Base conclusions on evidence and analysis
- Avoid bias in methodologies and reporting

## Professional Obligations

### Accept Only Qualified Engagements
- Take on assignments only within areas of competence
- Ensure adequate resources and expertise are available
- Decline work that cannot meet professional standards

### Use Best Practices
- Follow methodologies recognized by the Society
- Apply current industry standards and guidelines
- Stay current with evolving practices and regulations

### Maintain Client Confidentiality
- Protect sensitive client information
- Disclose information only with proper authorization
- Secure documentation and data appropriately

## Fee Standards

### Prohibited Fee Structures
**Contingent fees for federal tax depreciation services are strictly prohibited**

**Specifically Prohibited:**
- Fees based on percentage of "tax savings"
- Compensation tied to depreciation amounts identified
- Any arrangement where fee depends on study outcome

**Rationale:**
- Ensures objectivity in analysis
- Prevents bias in asset classification
- Maintains professional integrity
- Aligns with IRS expectations

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations
- All members must report suspected violations to the Ethics Committee
- Confidential reporting mechanisms available
- Duty to maintain professional standards

### Disciplinary Actions
The Ethics Committee may impose:
- **Probation:** Temporary supervision with conditions
- **Suspension:** Temporary removal of membership privileges
- **Permanent Removal:** Expulsion from the Society

**Process:**
- Investigation of reported violations
- Opportunity for member response
- Committee deliberation and decision
- Appeal procedures available
