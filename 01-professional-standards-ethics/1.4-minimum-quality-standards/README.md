# Minimum Quality Standards (MQS)

## Definition
The Minimum Quality Standards represent standard commercial and customary practices for the cost segregation process, encompassing both methodology and deliverable requirements.

## Purpose

### Define Standard Practices for Conducting Studies
- Establish consistent methodology across the industry
- Provide framework for the cost segregation **process**
- Ensure systematic approach to property analysis

### Provide In-Depth Description of Principal Elements
- Detail required components of the cost segregation **deliverable**
- Specify report structure and content requirements
- Define documentation standards

### Align with IRS Guidelines
- Incorporate relevant IRS audit technique guides
- Reflect Tax Court precedents and rulings
- Maintain consistency with federal tax regulations

## Objectives

### Consistent and Relevant Report Content Standards
- Standardize report formats and sections
- Ensure all reports contain essential elements
- Provide clarity for report users and reviewers

### Minimum Standards for Site Inspections
- Define scope of required site observations
- Establish documentation requirements for inspections
- Specify qualifications for inspection personnel

### Protocols for Documenting Research and Conclusions
- Require thorough documentation of analysis
- Establish standards for supporting evidence
- Define acceptable methodologies for asset classification

## Scope

### Site Observations and Documentation Reviews
- Physical property inspection requirements
- Review of construction documents and cost records
- Interviews with relevant parties
- Photographic documentation standards

### Engineering-Based Procedures
- Cost estimation methodologies
- Asset classification techniques
- Allocation methods and calculations
- Quality control processes

### Legal Frameworks for Quality Reports
- Compliance with tax law requirements
- Application of tangible property regulations
- Reference to relevant court cases and IRS guidance
- Documentation to support tax positions

### Complete Process Coverage
The MQS encompasses the entire cost segregation engagement:
- **Initial Phase:** Client engagement and data gathering
- **Inspection Phase:** Site visit and observation
- **Analysis Phase:** Engineering analysis and cost allocation
- **Reporting Phase:** Final deliverable preparation
- **Quality Review:** Certification and quality assurance

## Application
All ASCSP members conducting cost segregation studies must adhere to these Minimum Quality Standards. Reports certified by CCSPs must demonstrate full compliance with MQS requirements.
