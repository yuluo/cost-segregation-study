# Boddie-Noell Enterprises (Look and Feel)

## Case Citation

*Boddie-Noell Enterprises, Inc. v. Commissioner*, T.C. Memo 1996-336

## The Issue

This case addressed the classification of items that provide a specific "image" or "atmosphere" for a business. Whether decorative and aesthetic elements are structural components or personal property.

## Background

**Case Context:**
- Hardee's restaurant chain case
- Focused on whether decorative elements in restaurants were "structural" or "personal"
- Items included specialized lighting, wall coverings, millwork, and decorative finishes
- Taxpayer argued these created brand identity and customer experience

## Court's Analysis

**Focus:** If an item is easily removable and serves an ornamental or branding purpose rather than a structural one, it is generally ยง1245.

### Key Factors

**Removability:**
- Can items be removed without damaging structure?
- Removal method and difficulty
- Potential for reuse after removal

**Purpose:**
- Decorative/branding vs. structural support
- Customer experience vs. building function
- Business-specific vs. general building

**Replaceability:**
- Are items periodically updated for rebranding?
- Expected useful life vs. building life
- Remodel frequency patterns

**Business-specific:**
- Do items reflect specific brand identity?
- Unique to this business concept
- Would be removed if business changed

## Holdings

### Classified as Personal Property

**Decorative wall coverings and finishes:**
- Non-structural wallpaper, vinyl coverings
- Decorative paint schemes
- Removable wall panels
- Theme-specific finishes

**Non-structural millwork and trim:**
- Custom cabinetry (not load-bearing)
- Decorative moldings and trim
- Display elements
- Brand-specific built-ins

**Decorative lighting fixtures:**
- Specialty pendant lights
- Themed chandeliers
- Accent lighting
- Decorative sconces (not general lighting)

**Signage and branding elements:**
- Interior brand signage
- Logo elements
- Decorative graphics
- Theme markers

**Custom seating and booths:**
- If removable without structural damage
- Booth seating systems
- Custom furniture
- Dining area fixtures

### Classified as Structural

**Wall structure itself:**
- Framing and drywall
- Load-bearing elements
- Exterior walls
- Demising walls

**Ceiling grid and supports:**
- Suspended ceiling structure
- Support systems
- Ceiling tiles (unless decorative overlay)

**General lighting infrastructure:**
- Recessed can lighting
- Standard fluorescent fixtures
- General ambient lighting
- Building-wide lighting circuits

**Permanently affixed elements:**
- Items requiring demolition to remove
- Integrated structural components
- Non-removable installations

## The "Look and Feel" Concept

### Definition

The distinctive aesthetic and atmosphere that creates brand identity and customer experience.

### Characteristics

**Trade dress and brand standards:**
- Consistent brand identity across locations
- Franchise design requirements
- Corporate image standards
- Customer recognition elements

**Periodically updated for rebranding:**
- 5-7 year refresh cycles common
- Updated to reflect current branding
- Remodel programs
- Concept evolution

**Specific to business concept:**
- Not generic building improvements
- Unique to this brand/concept
- Creates differentiation
- Part of competitive strategy

**Not required for structural integrity:**
- Building functions without these elements
- Decorative rather than structural
- Aesthetic rather than functional
- Removable without building compromise

**Removable for remodel:**
- Designed to be updated
- Removal method documented
- Previous remodel history
- Expected to change over time

## Practical Considerations

### Removability Test

**Can element be removed without structural damage?**
- Method of attachment
- Removal process documented
- No structural compromise
- Building remains functional

**Would removal leave building fully functional?**
- Structural integrity maintained
- Building systems unaffected
- Code compliance continues
- Only aesthetic change

**Is removal practical for remodeling?**
- Reasonable time and cost
- Standard remodel practice
- Designed for replacement
- Previous remodel evidence

### Business Purpose Test

**Does element serve branding or customer experience?**
- Brand identity component
- Customer-facing purpose
- Competitive differentiation
- Marketing function

**Is element specific to this business concept?**
- Unique to brand/concept
- Not generic building improvement
- Franchise/corporate standards
- Business model driven

**Would element be removed if business concept changed?**
- Concept-specific rather than building-specific
- Expected to change with rebranding
- Not useful to different tenant
- Business, not building, asset

### Remodel Frequency

**Items updated every 5-7 years for rebranding:**
- Documented remodel cycles
- Corporate refresh programs
- Franchise requirements
- Industry standards

**Shorter useful life than building:**
- Economic obsolescence before physical
- Fashion and trend cycles
- Consumer preference changes
- Competitive pressure

**Reflects fashion/trend cycles:**
- Design trends evolve
- Brand evolution
- Market positioning
- Customer expectations

## IRS Position

**Generally accepts classification when properly supported:**
- Clear documentation required
- Reasonable methodology
- Industry standards followed
- Proper support provided

**Requires clear demonstration of removability:**
- Physical evidence
- Removal method documented
- Actual remodel history
- Practical removability

**Documentation of business purpose is critical:**
- Brand identity function
- Customer experience purpose
- Business necessity
- Competitive requirement

**Must distinguish from structural elements:**
- Clear separation
- Structural vs. decorative
- Permanent vs. temporary
- Building vs. business asset

## Key Takeaways

1. **Look and feel items can be personal property:** Decorative and brand-specific elements
2. **Removability is critical:** Must be removable without structural damage
3. **Business purpose matters:** Branding and customer experience justify classification
4. **Remodel frequency supports shorter life:** Regular refresh cycles indicate personal property
5. **Documentation is essential:** Clear support for removability and business purpose required
