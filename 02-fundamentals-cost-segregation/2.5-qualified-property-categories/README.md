# Qualified Property Categories (QIP, QLI, QRP, QRIP)

## Overview

Special tax rules apply to certain improvement types that may allow for even faster depreciation than standard MACRS. Legislative changes have modified these categories over time.

## Qualified Improvement Property (QIP)

**Created:** 2016 tax legislation

**Definition:**
- Interior improvements to non-residential buildings
- Must be made AFTER the building was originally placed in service

**Exclusions:**
- Enlargement of the building
- Elevators or escalators
- Internal structural framework

**Recovery Period:**
- Originally intended for 15-year life
- Technical correction made QIP eligible for bonus depreciation

## Historical Qualified Property Categories

### Qualified Leasehold Improvements (QLI)

- Improvements to interior portion of non-residential building
- Must be pursuant to a lease
- Generally 15-year recovery period

### Qualified Restaurant Property (QRP)

- Building improvements for restaurant use
- More than 50% of square footage for preparation/seating
- 15-year recovery period

### Qualified Retail Improvement Property (QRIP)

- Interior improvements to retail space
- Building must be at least 3 years old
- 15-year recovery period

## Section 179 Expensing Eligibility

### Qualified Improvements Include

- Roofs
- HVAC systems
- Fire protection and alarm systems
- Security systems

### Immediate Expensing

Eligible for Section 179 immediate deduction (subject to annual limits). Depends on:
- Current legislative limits (varies by year)
- Building type and use
- Taxpayer's total qualifying investment

## Strategic Importance

- Significant tax planning opportunity
- Requires careful analysis of improvement timing and building status
- May provide even greater benefits than standard cost segregation
