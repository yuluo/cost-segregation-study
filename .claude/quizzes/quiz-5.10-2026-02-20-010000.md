## Question 1
How many divisions were in the pre-2004 MasterFormat structure?

A) 10 divisions
B) 16 divisions
C) 23 divisions
D) 50 divisions

**Your Answer**: B
**Correct Answer**: B ✓
**Explanation**: The pre-2004 MasterFormat used **16 divisions** (Division 1 through Division 16). This structure was used from 1978 until the major revision in 2004. The 16-division format covered all construction work from General Requirements through Electrical, with Division 15 handling all Mechanical work and Division 16 covering all Electrical work. According to the web search, the last publication to include the 16-division format was the 1995 MasterFormat edition.

C) Divisions 23 and 26
D) Divisions 26 and 27

**Your Answer**: B
**Correct Answer**: B ✓
**Explanation**: The old pre-2004 **Division 15 (Mechanical)** was split into two separate divisions in the post-2004 structure: **Division 22 (Plumbing)** and **Division 23 (HVAC)**. ## Question 6

A) Reject the invoices as invalid since Division 15 doesn't exist in post-2004 MasterFormat
B) Maintain the contractor's Division 15 organization for reconciliation and document that the contractor used the pre-2004 structure
C) Immediately convert all Division 15 items to Divisions 22 and 23 before beginning the study
D) Request that the contractor re-issue all invoices using the post-2004 division structure

**Your Answer**: B
**Correct Answer**: B ✓
**Explanation**: The study material's best practices state: "Use the MasterFormat edition that matches available documentation" and "Maintain consistency throughout the study." Even though the project is from 2010 (after the 2004 revision), if the contractor organized their specifications and billing under the pre-2004 Division 15 structure, the analyst should maintain that organization for reconciliation purposes. The study material specifically recommends: "Identify which MasterFormat edition was used, Maintain original division structure for reconciliation, Document any conversion to current divisions." This preserves the audit trail and allows clear reconciliation between the study and contractor records.
