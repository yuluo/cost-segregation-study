## Question 1
How many primary cost segregation methodologies does the IRS recognize?

A) Four methodologies
B) Five methodologies
C) Six methodologies
D) Seven methodologies

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: C ✓
**Explanation**: The IRS recognizes six primary cost segregation methodologies: (1) Detailed Engineering Approach from Actual Cost Records, (2) Detailed Engineering Cost Estimate Approach, (3) Survey or Letter Approach, (4) Residual Estimation Approach, (5) Sampling or Modeling Approach, and (6) "Rule of Thumb" Approach.

## Question 4
A client purchased an existing commercial building 5 years ago with limited construction documentation available. Which methodology would likely be MOST appropriate for this scenario?

A) Detailed Engineering Approach from Actual Cost Records
B) Survey or Letter Approach
C) Residual Estimation Approach
D) Rule of Thumb Approach

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: C ✓
**Explanation**: The Residual Estimation Approach is most appropriate when dealing with existing property purchases where limited or no construction documentation is available. This method works by allocating the total purchase price across different asset categories using industry data, cost manuals, and engineering expertise. Since the client purchased an existing building with limited records, the Detailed Engineering Approach from Actual Cost Records (A) wouldn't be feasible. The Residual Estimation Approach allows the cost segregation professional to develop reasonable estimates even without original construction documentation.

## Question 6
What is a key best practice when applying IRS-recognized methodologies in cost segregation studies?

A) Always use only one methodology to maintain consistency
B) Use the Rule of Thumb Approach whenever possible for efficiency
C) Combine elements from multiple methodologies where appropriate
D) Avoid using the Detailed Engineering Approach due to its complexity

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: C ✓
**Explanation**: A key best practice in cost segregation studies is to combine elements from multiple methodologies where appropriate. Quality studies are not limited to a single approach; professionals should use the most detailed methodology that available documentation supports and may integrate techniques from different methods to achieve the most accurate and defensible results. For example, a study might primarily use the Detailed Engineering Approach from Actual Cost Records but incorporate the Residual Estimation Approach for components where specific records are missing. This flexible, comprehensive approach produces more reliable studies while ensuring all cost allocations reconcile to actual total project costs.
