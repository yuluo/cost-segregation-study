## Question 1
How many divisions did MasterFormat have in its pre-2004 structure?

A) 10 divisions
B) 16 divisions
C) 26 divisions
D) 50 divisions

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: B ✗
**Explanation**: The pre-2004 MasterFormat used 16 divisions (Division 1 through Division 16), covering categories from General Requirements through Electrical. The post-2004 revision expanded this to 50 divisions to provide more detailed organization, splitting older divisions like Division 15 (Mechanical) into Divisions 22 (Plumbing) and 23 (HVAC), and Division 16 (Electrical) into Divisions 26, 27, and 28.

## Question 3
What is the broadest (highest) level in the MasterFormat hierarchical numbering system?

A) Section
B) Subsection
C) Detail
D) Division

**Your Answer**: D
**Correct Answer**: D ✓
**Explanation**: Division is the broadest (Level 1) in the MasterFormat hierarchy. The four levels from broadest to most specific are: (1) Division (e.g., Division 09 - Finishes), (2) Section (e.g., 09 50 00 - Ceilings), (3) Subsection (e.g., 09 51 00 - Acoustical Ceilings), and (4) Detail (e.g., 09 51 13 - Acoustical Panel Ceilings). This hierarchical structure allows increasingly specific organization of project data.

## Question 6
A cost segregation analyst is reviewing contractor invoices for a manufacturing facility. She identifies dedicated electrical circuits installed specifically to power heavy CNC machinery. Under the post-2004 MasterFormat, which division would organize these circuits?

A) Division 13 (Special Construction)
B) Division 23 (HVAC)
C) Division 26 (Electrical)
D) Division 40 (Process Integration)

**Your Answer**: D
**Correct Answer**: C ✗
**Explanation**: Dedicated electrical circuits — even those serving heavy machinery — are organized under Division 26 (Electrical) in the post-2004 MasterFormat. Division 26 covers all electrical systems including service and distribution, branch wiring, lighting fixtures, and specialty systems. Division 40 (Process Integration) covers process piping and instrumentation systems, not electrical circuits. The cost segregation significance here is that these dedicated machine circuits may qualify as 5-7 year personal property rather than 39-year real property — but the MasterFormat organization still places them under Division 26.
