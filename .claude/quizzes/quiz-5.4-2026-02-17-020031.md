## Question 1
What is the primary focus of the Residual Estimation Approach in cost segregation?

A) Identifying and estimating costs for all building components individually
B) Identifying costs of short-lived assets (5-year and 7-year property) only
C) Calculating the fair market value of the entire property
D) Estimating land value separately from building costs

**Your Answer**: A
**Correct Answer**: B ✗
**Explanation**: The Residual Estimation Approach is "an abbreviated method that focuses only on identifying the costs of short-lived assets (e.g., 5 or 7-year personal property)." Unlike detailed engineering methods that individually analyze all building components, the residual approach only identifies and estimates the 5-year and 7-year property costs. The remaining amount (the "residual") is then assigned to real property without detailed analysis. This is what makes it an abbreviated or simplified approach compared to comprehensive methodologies.

## Question 2
According to the Residual Estimation formula, how is the 39-year real property cost calculated?

A) Total Project Cost ÷ (Number of Asset Categories)
B) Total Project Cost - Personal Property (5-year + 7-year)
C) Total Project Cost × Percentage from IRS tables
D) Personal Property Costs × Residual Factor

**Your Answer**: B
**Correct Answer**: B ✓
**Explanation**: The study material provides the explicit formula: "Real Property (39-year) = Total Project Cost - Personal Property (5-year + 7-year)." This is the core calculation of the residual approach—personal property costs (5-year and 7-year assets) are totaled and subtracted from the total project cost, with the remaining "residual" amount assigned to the building and other long-lived assets. This subtraction method is what gives the approach its name.

## Question 3
What is identified as the primary weakness of the Residual Estimation Approach compared to detailed engineering methods?

A) It takes significantly more time to complete
B) It requires more expensive software and tools
C) Residual costs are generally not checked for reasonableness or reconciled to actual total project cost
D) It cannot be used for properties with personal property components

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: C ✓
**Explanation**: The study material explicitly states the primary weakness: "Less accurate than engineering approaches because residual costs are generally not checked for reasonableness or reconciled to the actual total project cost." The specific issues section further elaborates with "No validation: The residual amount is accepted without verification." This lack of validation and reconciliation is the fundamental flaw—the residual amount (which becomes the 39-year property allocation) is simply accepted as whatever remains after subtracting personal property costs, without verifying that this amount is reasonable for the actual building components.

## Question 4
A cost segregation study using the Residual Estimation Approach identifies $2 million in personal property (5-year and 7-year assets) from a total project cost of $10 million. What amount would be allocated to 39-year real property?

A) $2 million
B) $5 million
C) $8 million
D) $12 million

**Your Answer**: C
**Correct Answer**: C ✓
**Explanation**: Using the residual estimation formula: Real Property (39-year) = Total Project Cost - Personal Property (5-year + 7-year) = $10 million - $2 million = $8 million. This straightforward calculation demonstrates how the residual approach works—the personal property costs are subtracted from the total, and whatever remains (the "residual") is assigned to 39-year real property. In this case, $8 million would be allocated to the building and other long-lived assets without further detailed analysis of those components.

## Question 7
If you overestimate personal property costs by $500,000 in a Residual Estimation study, what is the direct impact on the real property allocation?

A) Real property is overstated by $500,000
B) Real property is understated by $500,000
C) Real property remains accurate because errors cancel out
D) The impact depends on the reconciliation process

**Your Answer**: B
**Correct Answer**: B ✓
**Explanation**: This demonstrates the "compounding errors" issue identified in the study material: "Errors in personal property estimation directly affect real property allocation." Using the formula Real Property (39-year) = Total Project Cost - Personal Property, if you overestimate personal property by $500,000, you're subtracting too much from the total project cost. This means the residual amount (real property) will be $500,000 less than it should be—it's understated. Unlike detailed methods with reconciliation and validation, the residual approach has "No validation: The residual amount is accepted without verification," so this error goes undetected and directly misallocates the costs.
