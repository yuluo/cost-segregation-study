# Certification of the Preparer

## Introduction

The certification of the preparer is a formal statement signed by the lead professional that establishes professional credibility and personal accountability for the study's findings and methodology. This certification is required by the American Society of Cost Segregation Professionals (ASCSP) Minimum Quality Standards and serves as critical documentation for audit defense, demonstrating that a qualified professional has taken personal responsibility for the work performed.

The certification transforms an impersonal report into a professional representation backed by individual credentials and reputation. It assures the client, their CPA, and the IRS that the study was prepared by a qualified professional applying appropriate standards and methodologies, and that the conclusions are supported by reasonable professional judgment based on actual site inspection and analysis.

## Preparer Certification Requirements

A complete preparer certification must include specific elements that demonstrate compliance with professional standards and establish the preparer's personal involvement and accountability.

**Required Statement Elements**

**Professional Standards Compliance**

The certification must affirmatively state that the study complies with applicable professional standards:

*ASCSP Minimum Quality Standards:*
"This cost segregation study has been prepared in accordance with the Minimum Quality Standards established by the American Society of Cost Segregation Professionals."

*Professional Engineering Standards:*
"The engineering analysis and methodology employed in this study comply with applicable professional engineering standards and practices."

*Tax Law and Regulations:*
"The tax and legal analysis applies current Internal Revenue Code provisions, Treasury Regulations, and relevant judicial precedents."

**Appropriate Methodology**

The certification must affirm that the methodology used is appropriate for the subject property:

*Methodology Appropriate for Property Type:*
"The methodology employed is appropriate for the property type, available documentation, and scope of engagement."

*IRS-Recognized Approach:*
"The study employs an IRS-recognized methodology as described in the IRS Audit Techniques Guide for Cost Segregation."

*Detailed Engineering or Other Approved Method:*
"A detailed engineering approach [or specify other method] has been applied, utilizing [quantity take-offs, actual costs, cost estimates, etc.]."

**Reasonable Assumptions**

Professional judgment acknowledgment:

*Conclusions Based on Reasonable Assumptions:*
"The conclusions presented in this study are based on reasonable professional assumptions given the available data and information."

*Estimates Supported by Available Data:*
"Where estimates were necessary due to incomplete data, such estimates are supported by engineering analysis, cost databases, and industry standards."

*Conservative Approach:*
"Where professional judgment was required, a reasonable and conservative approach was applied to ensure supportable and defensible positions."

**Personal Knowledge**

Critical affirmation of personal involvement:

*Site Inspection Personally Conducted or Supervised:*
"I [or my designated representative under my supervision] personally conducted a physical inspection of the subject property on [date]."

*Property Physically Observed:*
"The property was physically observed, including exterior, interior, building systems, and site improvements to the extent accessible."

*Client Information Reviewed and Verified:*
"Cost data, architectural and engineering drawings, and other information provided by the client have been reviewed and verified to the extent practicable."

**Independence Statement** (if applicable)

For studies where independence enhances credibility:

*No Financial Interest in Property:*
"I have no present or contemplated financial interest in the subject property."

*No Conflict of Interest:*
"I have no conflict of interest or bias concerning the property or the parties involved."

*Objective Analysis Performed:*
"The analysis has been performed objectively and impartially."

*Compensation Not Contingent:*
"My compensation for this engagement is not contingent upon the tax benefit derived from the study or the specific findings and conclusions."

This independence statement is particularly important for studies that may be challenged or for clients who desire the highest level of objectivity.

## Independent Review Certification (When Applicable)

For large, complex, or high-risk studies, an independent review adds credibility.

**When Required/Recommended**

*Large or Complex Studies:*
- Properties >$20M in value
- Highly complex properties
- Multiple buildings or phases
- Unusual property types

*Unusual Property Types:*
- First time handling specific property type
- Unique or specialized properties
- Limited precedent or guidance

*Aggressive Positions:*
- Classifications that may be challenged
- Novel legal positions
- Significant reclassifications

*Client Request:*
- Client desires additional assurance
- Client's CPA requests review
- Lending institution requirement

*Firm Quality Control Policy:*
- Firm requires review for all studies
- Peer review for certain thresholds
- Best practice for risk management

**Reviewer Qualifications**

*Senior Professional Credentials:*
- More experienced than preparer, or equivalent
- Strong credentials (PE, CCSP, CPA)
- Extensive cost segregation experience

*Independent of Preparer:*
- Not same person who prepared study
- Objective review perspective
- Fresh eyes on analysis

*Relevant Expertise:*
- Experience with similar property types
- Knowledge of applicable law and engineering
- Recognized expert in field

*CCSP Certification:*
- Preferred: CCSP certification
- Highest industry credential
- Demonstrates expertise

**Review Scope and Procedures**

*Comprehensive Study Review:*
- Review of all sections of report
- Schedules and calculations checked
- Supporting documentation examined

*Methodology Verification:*
- Appropriateness of methodology confirmed
- Application consistent with standards
- Engineering analysis validated

*Calculation Checking:*
- Sample calculations verified
- Schedules reconciled
- Mathematical accuracy confirmed

*Conclusions Evaluation:*
- Reasonableness of conclusions
- Supportability of classifications

## Circular 230 Compliance (For Tax Advisors)

Tax practitioners must comply with Circular 230 regulations governing practice before the IRS.

**Required Disclaimers**

*Tax Advice Limitations:*
"This cost segregation study constitutes tax advice regarding the proper classification and depreciation of property components. Any tax advice contained in this study was not intended or written to be used, and cannot be used, by any taxpayer for the purpose of avoiding penalties."

*Reliance Restrictions:*
If study is not intended as formal tax opinion, appropriate limitations on reliance should be stated.

*Scope of Engagement:*
"This study addresses depreciation classification and does not constitute advice regarding [list exclusions: state taxes, property taxes, other tax matters, etc.]."

**Standard of Practice Acknowledgment**

*Circular 230 Standards Followed:*
"As a practitioner authorized to practice before the Internal Revenue Service, I have complied with the standards of practice set forth in Treasury Department Circular 230."

*Professional Responsibilities Met:*
- Competence in subject matter
- Due diligence performed
- Reasonable basis for positions taken
- No unreasonable positions

**Limitations of Liability**

*Where State Law Permits:*
Some jurisdictions allow reasonable limitations on professional liability.

*Reasonable Limitations:*
- Limitation to amount of fees
- Exclusion of consequential damages
- Time limits for claims

*Client Acknowledgment:*
Client agreement to limitations (typically in engagement letter, not study).

**Tax Advice Disclosure Statements**

*Written Tax Advice Standards:*
If study constitutes written tax advice under Circular 230 ยง10.37, compliance with standards required.

*Covered Opinion Requirements:*
For certain tax opinions, heightened standards apply:
- Factual matters investigation
- Application of law to facts
- Conclusion regarding likelihood of success
- Limited scope opinion disclosures

*More-Likely-Than-Not Standard:*
If opinion is provided that positions are more likely than not to be sustained, detailed analysis required.

## Common Deficiencies

**Missing Preparer Certification Entirely:**
- Some reports lack any certification
- Fails ASCSP Minimum Quality Standards
- Undermines credibility
- No personal accountability

**Vague or Weak Certification Language:**
- Hedging language ("to the best of my knowledge")
- Qualified statements ("appears to be")
- Lack of affirmative commitment
- Unprofessional tone

**Insufficient Qualification Documentation:**
- Credentials not listed
- License numbers omitted
- Experience not documented
- No demonstration of expertise

**No Credentials Listed:**
- Anonymous preparer
- No license or certification disclosed
- Cannot verify qualifications
- Lacks credibility

**Unsigned Certification:**
- No signature present
- Electronic/stamped signature where original required
- Undated certification
- Not properly executed

**Boilerplate Without Property-Specific Acknowledgment:**
- Generic template language
- No reference to subject property
- Copy-and-paste obvious
- Not customized

The certification of the preparer serves as the personal professional representation that gives the cost segregation study its credibility and authority. A strong, complete certification by a well-qualified professional provides assurance to the client, their tax advisors, and the IRS that the study has been prepared competently, objectively, and in accordance with the highest professional standards. This personal accountability distinguishes quality professional work from impersonal, potentially unreliable analysis.
